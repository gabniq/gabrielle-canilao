Out of all the digital mediums that I create with, digital illustration has always been the one that I have the most experience with and hold dear to my heart. The above mini series, Complementary Versions, is a reflection of my experience in digital illustration, experimenting with color, subject, techniques, etc. As a whole, the mini series is focused on one major aspect of my art practice: color. Between the three works, there is a transition from a reddish-pink palette all the way into a greenish-blue one.

The first work, "In the Rose Field," heavily reflects on my interest in fashion and flowers, two things that I actively enjoy, and creates a pleasing piece where I am able to eminate elegance through fashion and roses. The second piece, "Shatter Me," takes a completely different route, allowing for a much more dramatic, tense energy through the textured brushstrokes, closer angle, and shattered glass effect atop it all. This is also the piece that acts as a transition through the mini series, experimenting with a gradient between two opposing, complementary colors. The last piece, "Passing By," emits a much more nostalgic, almost somber energy. Like "In the Rose Field," there is an emphasis on fashion and aesthetics, but in a completely different manor, and expresses movement in blurs and lights.
