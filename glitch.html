For this work, I set out to achieve the making of an album cover for one of my favorite albums, In Rainbows by Radiohead, through the remixing of a pre-existing image with a glitch effect. Inspired by one of the tracks on the album—Weird Fishes/Arpeggi—I sought out images of fish before coming across one of a beautifully lit aquarium. After doing so, I accessed the image as in its text data through the program Notepad++, where I was able to literally glitch the image through things such as deleting lines, adding random words/phrases (such as inserting song lyrics randomly throughout), and spacing out lines. This allotted for a lot of trial and error, as each change was practically random.

This process was the part that took the longest, as I when I first began doing this, I had a specific end image in mind that I aimed to achieve; however, because of the random nature of glitches, I did get frustrated when I did not get the exact final image I had in mind. At one point, I had to force myself to take a step back and remember that glitches are uncontrollable. Thus, I settled for going with the flow and ended up with a final glitched image that, while different from my original idea, I liked and felt represented the album well while keeping the aesthetic integrity of the original image.

For the last step, I took that image into Adobe Photoshop. From there, I experimented with color adjustments and cropping in order to create a more appealing image, centering on one fish in particular as well as adding the album title and artist. To finish it up, I overlaid a plastic texture and peeling price sticker in order to emulate the feeling that it is really an album cover, perhaps as a vinyl or cd.
