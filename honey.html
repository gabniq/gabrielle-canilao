The above animation, Honey, was made through inspiration from another of my passionsвҖ”music. The goal of the animation was to tell a story, illustrating my own personal connection to music and the ways in which it inspired my art practice, taking me to other places like in Honey. One major aspect of the work is the usage of color. In the animation, different colored scenes are used to illustrate the different "worlds," and color as a whole is a large consideration I have in my art practice, especially in my digital illustrations, but I wanted to carry it over to my animation as well, even though I still wanted to keep it monochrome.

Honey is the first long-span animation I have ever made, with all prior experience that I have had in animation being shorter clips, around 30 seconds or less. Each frame was hand-drawn on Medibang Paint Pro, then compiled in Adobe Animate, and finally edited in Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe After Effects. In using this frame-by-frame style, I learned a lot about managing time, as I forced myself to create a schedule in order to make sure that each frame was drawn not only before the deadline, but also with enough time for myself to create something that I can proudly show!
