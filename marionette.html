I may not be the biggest fan of creepy dolls, but the horror genre has always intrigued me—despite being a theme/genre that I hardly ever create in—so I challenged myself to try something new and go for a subtle horror energy in this work. Luckily, I was able to lean into things that I am familiar with—that being gothic fashion, dramatic lighting, and humanlike anatomy, to create my marionette.

To create Marionette, a model was made using Blender. In all honesty, I faced quite the learning curve when using it, as this was the first large-scale model that I had created with the program, and I had only had a couple of months of experience in it at the point where I had begun modeling. Creating the entire model was a months-long project, from blockout to detailing to final texturing and editing, but it was all worth it in the end, as I was able to create a final model that I am very happy with. And along the way, not was I able to pick up 3D modeling as another digital medium to create with, but the experience in 3D has actually assisted me greatly in developing my 2D practice.
