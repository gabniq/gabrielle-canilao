Off the Record

Off the Record is a published game solo developed by me. Aside from the free-to-use background music and a small code template I used, all other art, GUI, and coding was done by me. Off the Record was developed using the game engine Ren'py, while the art and assets (such as all the UI in the game) were made on a combination of Medibang Paint Pro and Adobe Photoshop. The final published game was posted on the game store platform itch.io for public download.

Video game development has always been something that I have been interested in, as I have been playing video games all my life! For Off the Record in particular, I chose the visual novel format as I wanted to focus on narrative and art, which I consider major parts of my own practice. This was especially important because I wanted to theme said narrative around music, which is another major inspiration for many of my other works.

In creating the game, I often found myself challenged by the programming of the game through Ren'py, as I already had limited experience in Python, but this was also my first time using Ren'py as a whole. However, with the support of the engine's online forum/community and a lot of trial and error, I was still able to complete the game, producing something that I can proudly say was worth the months of work. And despite all the new things I had to learn about Ren'py, game-making, and design as a whole, I actually really enjoyed the learning process! Being able to create something is one thing, but to see the fruits of my labor in not only a final product but also in my now familiarity with Python, game development, bug-fixing, etc. is very satisfying to see.

The 3 above images are screenshotted from the final version of the game, showing off multiple different screens and moments in-game. As mentioned, one thing that I had to create was the GUI for the game, such as the splash screen, various menus, textbox, etc. This was my first time doing so, and at the start, I was quite lost. Knowing the setting of the game to be primarily in a record store and revolving around music, I wanted the GUI to reflect that, from the vinyls in the splash screen and the textbox, the receipt-style sidebar, and even a small music charm on the phone. Additionally, coding these elements had taken much longer than I assumed as well. While I have had prior experience in graphic design through making logos and designing business cards, I had rarely done any UI design, let alone in Python. Thus, this portion of game developmentвҖ”designing and coding various GUI and buttons, had led to a wide amount of learning in not only what makes GUI successful, but also how to do it at all!

One of my favorite parts when it came to creating Off the Record was doing the character design and creating the various sprites used throughout the game. Above, the sprite sheet of both major characters (besides the player character) are shown, with their different expressions displayed. When designing either character, I made sure to utilize the fashion of either character to reflect their personalities. Fashion is something else that is important to me, as I do take care to express my sense of style through my outfits. As such, I had wanted to make sure both Nox and Naya had well-designed outfits that matched both personalities, from the alternative look for Nox to the softer, kid-like look for Naya.
