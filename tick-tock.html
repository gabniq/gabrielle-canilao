Tick Tock is a short film made in collaboration with a group of classmates of mineвҖ”Ethan, Rica, Sydney, and ShelbyвҖ”with the simple goal of generating anxiety with our clips. As such, we ended up leading into an unvoiced film, focusing on the natural sounds of things like a clock ticking or running feet, all relating to our experiences with Zoom classes.

In creating this, the version shown above is one fully edited by myself through both Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe After Effects. It was one of the first times I had ever used either program, only using Premiere once before and After Effects not at all. I found myself very stumped with working with either program, unused to the editing process and general workspace, feeling quite overwhelmed. Creating the final product had taken much longer than I had thought, and I ran into many difficulties, from accidentally deleting clips, having to learn how different tracks work, and how to create/use effects. In fact, I had reached a point to where I did very minimal editing in After Effects at all, frustrated with learning a new program; however, I still pushed through and completed what I set out to do. In the end, I searched quite a lot of questions and watched many tutorials on either program before I was finally able to complete the editing process. Despite my difficulties, I made sure to do my part in research what was necessary, and can proudly say that I am much more well-versed in either program!
