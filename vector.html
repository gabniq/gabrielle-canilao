One hobby that I enjoy is figurine collecting. As such, in experimenting with vector art, I wanted to include that hobby by choosing the subject of one of my favorite figurines (pictured on the right)! In creating this, I pushed my focus onto simplifying silhouettes and choosing clear shapes, and purposefully not making too many separate vector shapes in Adobe Illustrator, as I often find the point of vectors to be simplicity. Additionally, rather than using Illustrator's eyedropper tool for every shape, I slightly changed the color for some shapes, in order to simplify the piece even further, as I found that the exact eyedropped color would not work, as each shape contains multitudes of different colors. I also stuck to simplicity in using the gradient tool, only using it sparingly in the center, where a lot of attention is drawn, to retain the 3D rounded shape of the figurine. Lastly, after exporting the vector from Adobe Illustrator, I took it into Photoshop for minor adjustments, such as in the tone curve and values.
